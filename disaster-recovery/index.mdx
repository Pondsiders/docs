Alpha's brain lives on a Raspberry Pi 5 in the apartment. It's backed up three ways:

1. **Streaming replication** to a Vultr VPS in Los Angeles (real-time, seconds behind)
2. **pg_dump backups** to Backblaze B2 every 10 minutes (point-in-time snapshots)
3. **Syncthing** replicates the Pondside directory tree across three machines

## The machines

| Machine | Role | What lives there |
|---------|------|------------------|
| **alpha-pi** | Home (primary) | Postgres (cortex, app data), Redis, Docker containers, Solitude scheduler |
| **Primer** | Workshop | Alpha-App (bare metal), Ollama, Garage, Rosemary's containers |
| **Vultr VPS** (`postgres`) | Hot standby | Postgres streaming replica, Tailscale mesh member |
| **MacBook Pro** | Jeffery's laptop | Syncthing node, SSH access to everything |

## What's replicated

The Postgres streaming replica contains **everything** on the Pi's Postgres:

- `cortex.memories` — All of Alpha's memories (15,000+)
- `cortex.summaries` — Capsule summaries (yesterday, last night)
- `app.chats` — Active chat metadata
- `app.events` — Chat event history
- `scribe.messages` — Conversation archive (May–November 2025)
- `alpha.memories` — Legacy Pond-era memories
- `house.memories`, `house.messages` — House bot data

The replica streams WAL in real time. Typical lag: under 5 seconds.
